VIES REGISTRATION

You'll need a VIES number if a company is selling goods to a company based in a different EU country.

VIES Registration

If a company is selling goods to a company based in a different EU country and both companies are listed in the VIES/ROI database, then the goods are exempt from VAT tax.

If, on the other hand, both companies are based in the same country then VAT must still be applied.

Using the VIES database, an approved company can find out immediately whether an invoice must include VAT or not by looking up the VAT number of the company they are doing business with. Each EU member tax office maintains an electronic database that sends the information to the central VIES database.

Please note:

Note that once you are registered, you may be removed from the system at any time, often because you do not have any intra-EU transactions.

EORI REGISTRATION

If you plan to import products into Spain, then you will need an EORI number.

Eori Registration

If your company is going to import products into Spain from outside of the EU, then you should apply for a Spanish EORI number.

We will get your EORI number in just one day.

An EORI is a number, unique throughout the European Community , assigned by a customs authority in a Member State to economic operators (businesses) or persons.

Please note:

You should get your EORI number in the member state where you did your first import/export trading. This will normally be your home country. Once you obtain the EORI number, this will be valid for all EU member states.

Once you have applied and registered with the Spanish Tax Agency, you will have to wait for approximately 48 hours for it to be activated and the goods can then be released.

SOCIAL SECURITY & TAX INCOME

Why you should know your obligations when you have employees in Spain.

Social Security & Tax Income

If you work in Spain, you must pay Spanish taxes and register with the Spanish Social Security service. This registration is valid for life .

Foreigners who are living and working in Spain must register and pay their taxes to the Spanish social security system. This is in exchange for certain government benefits, including free Spanish healthcare.

  • Taxes are between a 30 % – 35% over the gross salary (with a limit of 1.310 €)
  • This amount is witheld from the employees payslip, which means that no cost represents for the company
  • Self-employed have to pay their own Spanish contributions in full, albeit at a lower total percentage

Please note:

As a general rule, you must be employed and be paying contributions in order to be covered for illness, injuries, and accidents at work, unemployment, maternity and paternity leave .

Tax Implications

Depending on the Community where the property is based, the tax rate is different. It goes from 6% (Madrid) up to 10% (Catalonia). This taxed is called ITP and is calculated on top of the property’s price .

INTERNATIONAL TAXATION

INCOME TAX / TAX PLANNING

If you are living and working in Spain, you are liable to pay income taxes in Spain on your income and assets.

Income Tax / Tax Planning

If you’re a resident of Spain, you must pay Spanish tax on your worldwide income. Taxes apply on a progressive scale, although tax deductions exist. If you are a non-resident in Spain, you only pay tax in Spain on Spanish income, typically at a flat rate.

Spanish tax also applies to property ownership, investment interest, and goods and services (VAT) in Spain. Here as some of them:

  • Income taxes
  • Wealth taxes
  • Inheritance taxes
  • Property taxes

You should know that taxes in Spain are split between state and regional governments.

This means that Spanish tax rates can vary across the country for income tax, property tax, wealth tax, capital gains tax, and inheritance tax in Spain. Furthermore, workers in Spain must contribute to Spanish social security taxes. The Spanish tax year runs from 1 January to 31 December .

BECKHAM LAW / WEALTH TAX

A special tax regime that enables foreigners who move to the Spanish territory to pay a flat fee of 24% only.

Beckham Law / Wealth Tax

So, basically, this special tax regime for expats make it possible to end up paying much lower taxes, hence being able to save huge amounts of money.

It allows all the workers who reside abroad that want to come to work in Spain to pay income and wealth tax as if they were non-residents during the first 6 years.

It was created to attract talent and qualified workers into Spain ; and got its name after the famous football player David Beckham (the first one to take advantage of it).

Under the Beckham law, no matter if you spend over 183 days in the country (hence becoming a tax resident), you will pay taxes under taxation rules similar tho those who are non-residents.

Now expats can pay much less during their first 6 years in the country . And they can save taxes provided that they apply for this regime.

As far as Wealth Tax is concerned, is a tax for high net worth residents in Spain .

Spain continues to be a favourite destination for expatriates, including those making the most of their retirement years. There are many benefits to living here, and with the wide variety of property and locations, there is a dream home for everyone.

Besides the expected income and capital gains taxes, Spain additionally imposes an annual wealth tax. While most people are exempt – in general, if you are resident in Spain, you can only be affected if the net value of your worldwide assets is over €1 million – it can have a significant impact on wealthy individuals.

GOLDEN VISA / BUSINESS VISA

Spain launched its golden visa programme in 2013. An investment of at least €500,000 is needed.

Golden Visa / Business Visa

If you are considering purchasing or investing in property in Spain for at least 500.000€ then you could get the residency in Spain for your entire family.

On the other hand, if you invested in Spain after 27th September 2013 then you could also apply for the residency in Spain including your family. This visa is the only one you can apply from Spain.

  • You could apply for the Golden Visa from your own country
  • You could also apply for the Golden Visa from Spain
  • The Spanish investor visa can be renewed every two years

After five years it is possible to gain permanent residency and after ten years citizenship.

It is not necessary to live in Spain in order to retain and renew the residency visa permit.

Wealth Visa

The visa helps ex-pats who want to stay in the country but don’t have a reliable income source. Among many of those immigrating to Spain, you may find individuals who may move into the country because of reasons that push them without proving that they have an income-generating activity.

However, the wealth visa in Spain is given to the people who demonstrate that they can sustain themselves effectively while in Spain.

NIE / TIE / RESIDENCY PERMITS

All property buyers or foreigners who wish to live in Spain must obtain a Spanish ID number (NIE).

NIE / TIE / Residency Permits

If you don't have a NIE, you can apply for it in person at the Spanish Consulate, or in any Oficina de Extranjeros in Spain.

NIE is an abbreviation for Número de Identidad de Extranjero, which translates as Identification Number for Foreigners, or Foreigners’ Identification Number if you prefer.

  • The NIE is your all-purpose identification and tax number in Spain
  • You need it for everything that involves an official filing in Spain
  • Without a NIE number, the authorities are unable to assess your annual tax payments
  • Anyone who wants to work in Spain, or start a business need a NIE.

Please note:

If you are an EU citizen and spend longer than 3 months in Spain after getting your NIE number, you are required to register and get a government certificate that shows your NIE number .
